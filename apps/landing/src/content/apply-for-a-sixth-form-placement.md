If you have completed CSEC and want to continue your studies at a public secondary school that offers Sixth Form (CAPE), use this service to apply.

There is no application fee.

## Who can apply

You can apply if you have completed CSEC examinations and want a Sixth Form placement at a public secondary school that offers CAPE.

Placement is not guaranteed. It depends on the availability of spaces and the entry requirements of each school. Each school sets its own minimum grades for each subject — typically at least 5 CSEC passes (Grades I, II or III) including English A and Mathematics.

If you are waiting on your results, you can still apply by selecting ‘Pending’ as the grade.

## Before you start

You will need documents ready to upload:

1. Your CSEC results slip or certificate.
2. Your birth certificate.
3. Your National ID, if you are 16 or over.

Apply by the deadline announced by the Ministry — usually within 4 weeks of CSEC results being released. Late applications may not be considered for the September intake.

## How to apply

It takes about 15 minutes to apply online.

## What the form asks for

1. Your name, sex, date of birth, National ID number, home address, parish, mobile number and email address.
2. The school you attended for CSEC.
3. Your CSEC subjects and grades.
4. Your parent or guardian’s relationship to you, name, mobile number and email address.
5. Your Sixth Form school choices, in order of preference.
6. The CAPE subjects you would like to study.
7. Your supporting documents.
8. A declaration confirming the information you have given is true and correct.

You can upload files in PDF, JPG, PNG or DOC format, up to 10 MB each.

Each school offers its own subject combinations, known as ‘option blocks’. Your choices show the Ministry what you would like to study, but the school you are placed at may not offer that exact mix.

## After you apply

You will get a reference number when you submit your application.

1. The Ministry reviews your application against each chosen school’s entry requirements.
2. Each school confirms which students it can take.
3. You will be told your placement by letter and email before the September intake.
4. Once you are placed, the school confirms which CAPE subjects are available.
