## Who can register

You can register as a private candidate if you are not registered for CSEC through a school. You can choose when to take the exam and the subjects you want to take.

## Before you start

Registration deadlines are set by CXC, and late registration is not accepted. Check the CXC website for this year’s deadline before you start.

CXC charges a fee for each subject. You will be told the fees due after you submit your registration.

## How to register

You can apply online. It takes about 10 to 20 minutes to complete, depending on the number of subjects you are entering.

## What the form asks for

- your name, date of birth, sex and National ID number
- your home address, telephone number and email address
- which sitting you prefer (January or May/June)
- how many subjects you want to enter
- the subject name, whether you are taking an alternative paper, and whether it is a resit. If it is a resit, you must also give your previous registration number
- a declaration confirming your information is accurate, that you have checked for any timetable clashes, and that you’ll tell the registrar if your name changes

## After you register

The CXC Local Registrar will check your registration and confirm your candidate number and the fees due. You will then receive instructions on how to pay. Your timetable and examination centre details are sent closer to the examination date. A confirmation email is also sent to the email address you provided.

If you need help, contact the CXC Local Registrar at 535-0600 or by email at examssection@mes.gov.bb
