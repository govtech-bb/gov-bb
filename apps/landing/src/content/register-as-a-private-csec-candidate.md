If you are not registered for the Caribbean Secondary Education Certificate (CSEC) through a school, you can register as a private candidate. Use this service to choose your sitting and enter the subjects you want to sit.

## Who can register

This service is for people sitting CSEC as private candidates, rather than through a school.

## Before you start

Registration deadlines are set by CXC each year, and late registration is not accepted. Check the CXC website for this year’s deadline before you start.

CXC charges a fee for each subject. You’ll be told the fees due after you submit your registration.

You’ll need about 10 to 20 minutes to complete the form, depending on how many subjects you are entering.

## How to register

## What the form asks for

1. Your personal details: name, date of birth, sex and National ID number.
2. Your contact details: home address, telephone number and email address.
3. Which sitting you want: January or May/June.
4. How many subjects you want to enter.
5. For each subject: the subject name, whether you are taking an alternative paper, and whether it is a resit. If it is a resit, you’ll also give your previous registration number.
6. A declaration confirming your information is accurate, that you have checked for any timetable clashes, and that you’ll tell the registrar if your name changes.

## After you register

The CXC Local Registrar reviews your registration and confirms your candidate number and the fees due. You’ll then get instructions on how to pay. Your timetable and exam centre details are sent closer to the exam date, and a confirmation email is sent to the address you gave.

If you need help, you can contact the CXC Local Registrar by phone on +1 (246) 535-0600 or by email at cxc.local.registrar@mes.gov.bb.
