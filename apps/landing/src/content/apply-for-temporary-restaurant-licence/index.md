Use this service to apply for a licence to operate a temporary restaurant — for example, to serve food at an event.

You need a licence if you will serve any food or drink at an event, whether you sell it or give it away, including free samples. Both the event organiser and each food vendor must apply.

Read [what counts as a temporary restaurant, who must apply, and what your event needs](/business-trade/temporary-restaurants-what-you-need-to-know).

## When to apply and what it costs

Apply at least 14 days before your event. Applications made later than this may not be accepted.

There is no application fee. If you are organising the event, you must pay any overtime costs for the environmental health officers attending.

## Apply online

You should complete your application in one go. At the moment, it is not possible to save your answers and come back to them later.

<a data-start-link href="/business-trade/apply-for-temporary-restaurant-licence/start">Start now</a>

You can also apply in person at your nearest polyclinic while we introduce this online service.

## What happens next

- The Environmental Health Department for your area will review your application.
- If you do not already have a licensed food business, an officer will inspect your set-up before the licence is issued.
- You must collect your licence from the polyclinic before your event.
- The licence is valid for 30 days from the date it is issued.
