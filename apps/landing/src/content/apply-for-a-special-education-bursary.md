If your child has additional learning needs that cannot be met in a government school, you can apply for a bursary towards their education at a private special-education provider in Barbados.

The bursary covers part of the annual fees. It is means-tested and discretionary, and the exact amount is decided case by case by the Bursary Committee based on your child’s needs and your household circumstances.

## Who can apply

You can apply if:

- your child is a Barbadian citizen or legally resident in Barbados
- your child has a documented diagnosis or assessed special education need
- a government-school placement is not suitable for your child’s needs
- a private special-education provider in Barbados has agreed to take your child

If approved, the bursary covers one school year. You must reapply each year your child continues in the placement.

## Before you start

It takes about 20 to 30 minutes once you have your documents. You’ll need to upload:

1. Your letter explaining the need for the bursary.
2. A medical or educational evaluation describing your child’s diagnosis or needs.
3. The provider’s acceptance letter confirming your child has been offered a place.

You can also include proof of income for the past 3 months, but this is optional.

## What the form asks for

You’ll need to give:

1. Your child’s details — their name, date of birth, sex and National ID number.
2. Your child’s needs — their diagnosis or assessed needs, their current school or programme, and why a private provider is needed.
3. Your details as the parent or guardian — your name, National ID number, relationship to the child, home address, phone number and email address.
4. Your household circumstances — the number of people in your household, the number of dependent children, your employment status, your approximate monthly household income, and any other government support you receive for this child.
5. The private provider’s details — their name, address, phone number, the annual fees, the amount you are requesting and the expected start date.
6. Your bank or credit union account details.

You’ll also need to confirm that your information is true and correct, that approval is discretionary and not guaranteed, and that you’ll report any changes in your child’s placement or your household circumstances.

## After you apply

A bursary officer reviews your application and may call you for more information. Your application is then presented to the Bursary Committee. A decision can take up to 8 weeks.

If approved, the bursary is normally paid directly to the provider on your behalf. In some cases it can instead be paid into your bank account. You’ll get a copy of your application by email.

If you have not heard anything within 8 weeks, you can call +1 (246) 535-0760 or email specialed@mes.gov.bb.
