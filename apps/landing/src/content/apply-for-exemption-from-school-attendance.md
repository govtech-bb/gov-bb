If your child cannot attend school in the usual way — for example, because of a medical condition, a disability, religious reasons, or an extended family relocation — you can apply for a Certificate of Exemption under the Education Act.

There is no fee.

An exemption from attendance is not an exemption from education. Your child must continue to receive education during the exemption period.

## Who can apply

You can apply if you are the parent or guardian of a child who cannot attend school in the usual way.

You can apply for one of the following reasons:

1. A medical or health condition.
2. A disability that requires an alternative education arrangement.
3. A family relocation or extended travel.
4. A religious or cultural reason.
5. Home schooling — this needs a separate Home Schooling application.
6. Another reason, which you describe.

An exemption is granted at the Ministry’s discretion and may be revoked if the conditions of the certificate are not met.

## Before you start

You will need documents ready to upload:

1. The child’s birth certificate.
2. A medical report, if you are applying for a medical reason.
3. Any other supporting document.

You can upload files in PDF, JPG, PNG or DOC format, up to 10 MB each.

## How to apply

It takes about 15 minutes to apply online.

## What the form asks for

1. Your title, name, National ID number, relationship to the child, home address, parish, mobile number and email address.
2. The child’s name, date of birth, sex and National ID or birth certificate number, along with their current school, last class and last date attended if they have attended school.
3. The reason you are applying and a description of your circumstances.
4. The start and end dates of the exemption you are requesting.
5. A description of how the child’s education will continue during the exemption.
6. Your supporting documents.
7. A declaration confirming the information you have given is true and correct.

## After you apply

You will get a reference number when you submit your application.

1. An Education Officer reviews your application and may ask for more information.
2. The Chief Education Officer approves or declines the application.
3. You will be told the outcome within 6 weeks by letter and email.

You must tell the Ministry promptly if your child’s circumstances change during the exemption period.
