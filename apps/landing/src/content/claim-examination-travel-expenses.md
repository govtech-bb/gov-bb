If you are a teacher or a member of exam-centre staff, you can claim reimbursement for travel you made on official examination business. This includes travel for invigilation, marking sessions, exam centre setup, and collecting or returning papers.

## Who can claim

If you are a teacher or examination centre staff member, you can claim payment for travel costs made on official examination business. This includes travel for invigilation, marking sessions, examination centre setup, and collecting or returning papers.

## Before you start

There is no fee to make a claim. You must submit one claim per month, and each form must cover one month of travel.

You do not need receipts for mileage claims, but you must use the published Civil Service mileage rate. For other expenses, you must upload your receipts.

## How to claim

You can claim online. The form takes about 10 to 15 minutes, depending on how many trips you are claiming for.

## What the form asks for

- your personal details (surname, first names, National ID number, school or examination centre, home address, mobile number and email address)
- travel period (the month and year you are claiming for)
- trip details (number of trips, and for each trip the date, where you travelled from and to, the purpose, and the amount claimed in Barbados dollars)
- receipts (supporting receipts where required for non-mileage expenses)
- declaration (confirmation that the trips were made on official examination business, that the information provided is correct, and that you understand the penalty for false information)

## After you claim

Your claim will be reviewed and approved by the Examinations Officer, Deputy Permanent Secretary and Accounts Officer.

You can expect payment through your school payroll within 6 weeks. A confirmation email will be sent to the email address you provided.

If you need help, contact the Ministry of Educational Transformation at 535-0600.
