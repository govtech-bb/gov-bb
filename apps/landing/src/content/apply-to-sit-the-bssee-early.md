If your child is academically ready to take the Barbados Secondary Schools’ Entrance Examination (BSSEE) ahead of their year group, for example at age 10 instead of 11, you can apply for them to sit it early.

Your child will be given only one opportunity to write the examination. If they sit early and do not do well, they cannot re-sit the BSSEE.

## Who can apply

You can apply if you are the parent or legal guardian of a primary school child and:

- your child is at least 9 years old on 31 August
- your child is currently enrolled in primary school, from Infants A through to Class 4
- your child’s school Principal supports the application

## Before you start

Apply before the BSSEE registration deadline, usually in late January.

Decisions are made by the Ministry and are at its discretion.

## How to apply

You can complete the online form. It takes about 10 minutes.

## What the form asks for

1. Your details as the parent or guardian, including your title, name, National ID number, home address, mobile number, email address and your relationship to the child.
2. Your child’s name and date of birth.
3. Which BSSEE sitting year you are applying for.
4. Your child’s current school and class.
5. Why your child is ready to sit early, and a summary of your supporting evidence.

You must upload the Principal’s recommendation letter. You can also upload test scores or school reports and any other supporting document.

You confirm that the information is true, that you understand your child has only one examination attempt, that the Principal supports the request, and that the decision rests with the Ministry.

## After you apply

You will get a copy of your application at the email address you provide.

The Ministry reviews your request and may consult your child’s primary school. You will be told the outcome by letter and phone within 4 weeks. If approved, your child is registered for that year’s BSSEE.
