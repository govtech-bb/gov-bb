Get ready for hurricane season and stay up to date with Department of Emergency Management (DEM) alerts. Hurricane season in Barbados runs from 1 June to 30 November. Prepare before the season starts — do not wait for a storm warning.

Use the household checklist on this page to make sure you are ready. It covers water, food, documents, first aid, communication, and the steps to take before a storm.

## Water

- Store at least 3 gallons of drinking water per person, for 3 days.
- Store 1 gallon of water per pet per day.
- Fill bathtubs and large buckets before the storm (Barbados Water Authority advice).
- Keep water purification tablets or bleach for emergencies.

After the storm, do not drink tap water until the Barbados Water Authority (BWA) says it is safe. Boil all drinking water for at least 1 minute.

## Food

- At least 3 days of non-perishable food, such as tinned goods, dry crackers and peanut butter.
- A manual can opener.
- Disposable plates, cups and forks.
- Baby formula and food, if you have an infant.

## Important documents

Keep these in a waterproof bag or container:

- Your national ID card or passport.
- Insurance documents (home, health and vehicle).
- Birth certificates for everyone in your household.
- Prescription details and medical records.
- Cash in small bills, as ATMs may not work after the storm.

## Health and first aid

- A first aid kit with plasters, bandages and antiseptic.
- A 7-day supply of prescription medicines.
- Hearing aids with spare batteries, and glasses or contact lenses with enough solution.
- Nappies, wipes and formula if you have a baby.
- Hand sanitiser and face masks.

## Communication

- A battery-powered or wind-up radio to hear DEM updates.
- A fully charged mobile phone and a portable power bank.
- An emergency contact list written on paper, not just stored on your phone.
- A whistle to signal for help if you get trapped.

## Shelter, tools and safety

- Torches with spare batteries.
- Candles and waterproof matches or a lighter.
- Sturdy shoes or boots, rain gear, and bedding for each person.
- A change of clothes for 3 days.
- Tools to turn off gas and water at the mains.
- Heavy-duty rubbish bags.

## Before the storm hits

- Board up or shutter windows and glass doors.
- Bring in outdoor furniture, plants and loose items.
- Fill your vehicle with petrol.
- Charge all devices, including phones, tablets and power banks.
- Let friends or family know your shelter plan, and make a plan for your pets.
- Arrange help for any elderly or disabled neighbours.

## Key contacts during a storm

Lines can be busy during a storm — keep trying.
