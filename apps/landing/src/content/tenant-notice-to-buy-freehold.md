If you rent a lot in a tenantry, the Tenantries Freehold Purchase Act (Cap. 239B) gives you the right to buy the freehold of that lot from your landlord. The first step is to send your landlord a written notice. This page tells you how to use Form 1 to do that.

## When you can use this form

Use Form 1 if you are a **tenant of a lot in a tenantry** and you want to start the process of buying the freehold of that lot.

This form is the notice required under section 12(1) of the Tenantries Freehold Purchase Act.

## What you need before you start

You will need:

- the **name of the person or company who owns the tenantry** (your landlord)
- the **name of the tenantry** your lot is part of
- the **parish** the tenantry is in
- the date you are signing the notice

You sign the notice yourself, as the tenant.

## Get legal advice

Buying a freehold has long-term legal and financial effects. Before you send the notice, you may want to speak to a lawyer, especially if your landlord disputes your right to buy or if there is more than one person living on the lot.
