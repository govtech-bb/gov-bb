The National Housing Corporation (NHC) sells land and homes to people who do not already own land or property. Use this service to apply to buy an NHC lot or house.

## Who can apply

You can apply if you **do not already own land or property**. NHC does not sell to people who already own land or a home.

You can apply on your own, or with a co-applicant, such as a partner or family member who will live in the home with you.

## What you can apply for

You can apply to buy:

- a 2-bedroom or 3-bedroom NHC home
- a lot to build on (low-income lots are usually 3,000 to 4,000 square feet)

Homes are built in timber, wall, timber-and-wall, or steel frame. You can say which type you prefer.

NHC may offer you a property in a different parish from the one you ask for, depending on what is available.

## What you need before you start

You will need:

- your National Registration Number (NRN)
- your TAMIS number
- contact details we can reach you on
- details of your employer, salary, and pay period
- the number of adults and children in your household, and the children's ages
- details of anyone in the household who has a disability
- details of any other income in your household, such as maintenance, alimony, or a second job

You will also need **one of the following**, depending on how you are paying:

- a **bank statement**, if you are paying in cash
- a **mortgage certificate**, if you are using a mortgage or loan

If you already have land and want NHC to build on it, and you are a tenant of that land, you will also need a **letter of permission from your landlord**.
