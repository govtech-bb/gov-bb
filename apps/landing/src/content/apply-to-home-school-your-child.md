Use this service to apply for permission to home-school your child in Barbados. Permission must be granted by the Permanent Secretary, Ministry of Educational Transformation.

There is no application fee.

## Who can apply

You can apply if you are the parent or guardian of a child you want to home-school.

## Before you start

You will need to:

1. Follow the National Curriculum or an alternative approved curriculum.
2. Attend an in-person interview before permission can be granted.
3. Provide regular progress reports for each child.

You will also need documents ready to upload, including a timetable that covers English, Mathematics, Science, Social Studies and Physical Activity, and certification for each tutor.

## How to apply

It takes about 20 minutes to apply online.

## What the form asks for

1. Your name, address, phone number, email address and residency status in Barbados.
2. How many children you want to home-school, and each child’s full name and date of birth.
3. Whether any of the children attended school before, and if so the school’s name and address.
4. How many tutors will teach the children, and each tutor’s name and qualifications.
5. Your reasons for home-schooling, including your plans for socialisation, sports and learning outside the home.
6. Documents to upload — a timetable and tutor certification. You can also upload an alternative curriculum and previous progress reports if you have them.
7. A declaration confirming the information you have given is true and correct.

You can upload files in PDF, JPG, PNG or DOC format, up to 10 MB each.

## After you apply

You will get a reference number when you submit your application.

The Administrative Officer in the Nursery and Primary Section will review your application and call you to arrange an interview within 3 weeks. The interview helps the Ministry decide whether to grant permission.

The Permanent Secretary grants or refuses written permission after the interview. A copy will be emailed to you once your application has been processed.
