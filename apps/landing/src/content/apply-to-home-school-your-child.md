Use this service to apply for permission to home-school your child in Barbados. Permission must be granted by the Permanent Secretary, Ministry of Educational Transformation.

There is no application fee.

## Who can apply

You can apply if you are the parent or guardian of a child you want to home-school.

## Before you start

You will need to:

- follow the National Curriculum or an alternative approved curriculum.
- attend an in-person interview before permission can be granted.
- provide regular progress reports for each child.

You will also need documents ready to upload, including a timetable that covers English, Mathematics, Science, Social Studies and Physical Activity, and certification for each tutor.

## How to apply

It takes about 20 minutes to apply online.

## What the form asks for

- your name, address, phone number, email address and residency status in Barbados.
- how many children you want to home-school, and each child’s full name and date of birth.
- whether any of the children attended school before, and if so the school’s name and address.
- how many tutors will teach the children, and each tutor’s name and qualifications.
- your reasons for home-schooling, including your plans for socialisation, sports and learning outside the home.
- documents to upload — a timetable and tutor certification. You can also upload an alternative curriculum and previous progress reports if you have them.
- a declaration confirming the information you have given is true and correct.

You can upload files in PDF, JPG, PNG or DOC format, up to 10 MB each.

## After you apply

You will get a reference number when you submit your application.

Our Nursery and Primary Section will review your application and call you to arrange an interview within 3 weeks. The interview helps the Ministry decide whether to grant permission.

The Permanent Secretary grants or refuses written permission after the interview. A copy will be emailed to you once your application has been processed.
