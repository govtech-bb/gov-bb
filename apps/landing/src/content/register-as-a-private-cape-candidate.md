## Who can register

If you are not registered for the Caribbean Advanced Proficiency Examination (CAPE) through a school, you can register as a private candidate. You can use this service to choose the subjects you want to take.

## Before you start

CXC sets registration deadlines, and late registration is not accepted. Check the CXC website for this year’s deadline before you start.

CXC charges a fee for each subject. You will be told the total fees due after you submit your registration.

## How to register

You can apply online. It takes about 10 to 20 minutes to complete, depending on the number of subjects you are entering.

## What the form asks for

- your name, date of birth, sex and National ID number
- your home address, telephone number and email address
- the number of subjects you are registering for

For each subject:
- subject name
- unit (unit 1 or unit 2)
- whether you are taking an alternative paper
- whether it is a resit
- previous registration number (if it is a resit)
- a declaration confirming that your information is accurate, that you have checked for timetable clashes, and that you will notify the registrar if your name changes

## After you register

The CXC Local Registrar will check your registration and confirm your candidate number and the fees due. You will then receive instructions on how to pay. Your timetable and examination centre details are sent closer to the examination date, and a confirmation email is sent to the email address you provided.

If you need help, contact the CXC Local Registrar at 535-0600 or by email at examssection@mes.gov.bb
