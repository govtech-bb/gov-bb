If your 11-year-old child is not yet ready to sit the Barbados Secondary Schools’ Entrance Examination (BSSEE), use this form to request a one-year deferral.

A deferral means your child sits the exam the following year. Your child will be given only one opportunity to write the examination.

## Who can apply

You can apply if you are the parent or guardian of a child who is around 10 to 12 years old on 31 August.

## Before you start

Submit your request before the BSSEE registration window closes, usually in late January.

Approval is at the Ministry’s discretion.

## How to apply

You can complete the online form.

## What the form asks for

1. Your child’s current primary school, name and date of birth.
2. Your details as the parent or guardian, including your title, name, address, contact number and email address.
3. Your reason for the deferral, such as low academic performance in Mathematics or English, missed instruction due to trauma or illness, or significant learning difficulties.
4. A written explanation of why your child is not ready.

You can also upload supporting documents, such as a medical letter or a school report or assessment. Each file can be a PDF, JPG, PNG or DOC and up to 10 MB.

You confirm that the information is true and correct, that you understand your child has only one opportunity to write the examination, and that approval is at the Ministry’s discretion.

## After you apply

You will get a confirmation email at the address you provide.

The Ministry will review your request, consult your child’s primary school where needed, and respond within 4 weeks. The decision is communicated by letter and phone.

If your request is approved, your child is automatically registered for next year’s exam. If it is denied, your child sits the exam as originally scheduled.
