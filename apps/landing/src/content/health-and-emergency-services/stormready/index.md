Get ready for hurricane season and stay up to date with Department of Emergency Management (DEM) alerts.

<notice>
<strong>Hurricane season</strong> runs from <strong>1 June to 30 November 2026</strong>. Prepare before the season starts — do not wait for a storm warning.
</notice>

## Household checklist

Use this checklist to make sure your household is ready for a hurricane. It covers water, food, documents, first aid, communication, and the steps to take before a storm.

### Highlights from the checklist

<highlights>
<highlight title="Water">Store at least 3 gallons of drinking water per person for 3 days. Fill baths, buckets and other containers before a storm.</highlight>
<highlight title="Documents">Keep your national ID, insurance documents and birth certificates in a waterproof bag. Have cash in small bills available.</highlight>
<highlight title="Communication">Charge all devices and power banks. Keep a battery-powered radio to receive DEM updates. Write down important phone numbers and emergency contacts.</highlight>
<highlight title="Before a storm">Board up windows if needed, bring in outdoor items, fill your vehicle with gas, and make sure family members know your emergency plan and shelter arrangements.</highlight>
</highlights>
