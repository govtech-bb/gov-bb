Search for an emergency shelter to use in the event of a hurricane or tropical storm. This service is for anyone in Barbados, including residents and visitors. It is free and you do not need to sign in.

Shelters open only when the Department of Emergency Management (DEM) announces that they are ready, during the Atlantic hurricane season (1 June to 30 November). Stay at home, or with family or friends, if it is safe to do so.

## How to find a shelter

You can search for shelters online to:

- find shelters in your parish
- see which shelters have accessible bathrooms or potable water
- see which shelters are used during or after a hurricane or storm

You can also:

- listen to local radio or watch TV news for the official list of open shelters
- follow the Department of Emergency Management, the Ministry of Educational Transformation, and the Barbados Government Information Service (BGIS) on social media
- call the Department of Emergency Management at [438-7575](tel:+12464387575)

## What to bring (Go Bag)

Pack a Go Bag before you go to a shelter, including:

- bottled water
- your ID
- any prescription medication
- a flashlight
- some cash

## Shelter rules

Before you go, know that:

- pets, alcohol, firearms and other weapons are not allowed in shelters
- smoking is not allowed inside shelters
- the space is shared, so be considerate of other occupants and follow the warden's instructions
- you are responsible for your belongings

## If you need help now, call

- Police: [211](tel:211)
- Fire Service: [311](tel:311)
- Ambulance: [511](tel:511)
- Department of Emergency Management: [438-7575](tel:+12464387575)
