If your child has been allocated to a secondary school you would like to change after the BSSEE results have been published, you can ask the Ministry to reallocate them.

Reallocation depends on space being available and is not guaranteed.

## Who can apply

You can apply if you are the parent or guardian of a child who received a BSSEE allocation and you want to request a different school.

## Before you start

Submit your request within the window the Ministry announces after the BSSEE results, usually 2 to 3 weeks. Late requests will not be considered for the September intake.

## How to apply

You can complete the online form.

<a data-start-link href="/education/request-a-bssee-school-reallocation/start">Start now</a>

## What the form asks for

1. Your details as the parent or guardian, including your title, name, National ID number, relationship to the child, home address, parish, mobile number and email address.
2. Your child’s name, date of birth, sex and primary school attended.
3. Your child’s BSSEE composite score and candidate number.
4. Your child’s current school allocation.
5. Your first-choice school for reallocation, and a second choice if you have one.
6. Your reason for the reallocation.

You must upload your child’s BSSEE results slip and birth certificate. You can also add notes and any other supporting document.

You confirm that the information is accurate, that you understand reallocation is not guaranteed, and that you accept the original allocation if your request is declined.

## After you apply

You will get a reference number straight away.

You will be told the outcome by letter and email before the September intake. If your request is approved, the new allocation replaces the original. If it is declined, the original allocation stands.
