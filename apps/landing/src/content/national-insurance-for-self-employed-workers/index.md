## Who this is for

This guidance is for you if you earn money from work and no employer pays National Insurance for that work. For example, if you:

- drive or deliver for an app
- work in a trade, beauty or personal care
- sell food, goods or crafts
- freelance, create content or do online work
- rent your place to visitors
- do domestic, casual or cash-in-hand work

You do not need a registered business or a work permit. You can also contribute for work you do on the side, even if an employer pays National Insurance for your main job.

## How much to pay

How much you pay is based on your earnings after business expenses. NIS sets a minimum and maximum amount each year. The amount you contribute affects how much you may receive when you claim a benefit.

Answer a few questions about your earnings and see an estimate of what you could pay and the benefits it could protect.

## Make a payment

Making contributions when you work for yourself is voluntary. If you choose to contribute, the full amount for the year must be paid by **15 January of the following year**. You can pay weekly, monthly or as one payment for the year.

Have your National Insurance number ready. You can pay:

- online through SurePay
- online through EZpay+
- through a bank
- in person at the NIS office

For the practical steps — getting your number, registering, paying and checking your record — see [how to get your benefits](/money-financial-support/national-insurance-for-self-employed-workers/how-to-get-your-benefits).

## Before you pay

Making contributions when you work for yourself is **voluntary**. If you choose to contribute, you need a National Insurance number and must be registered with NIS as self-employed.

**Having a National Insurance number does not mean you are already registered as self-employed.** If you have worked in Barbados before, NIS can check whether you already have a number.

You can [register as self-employed on the NIS website](https://www.nis.gov.bb/self-employment-registration/). NIS does not share your information with the Barbados Revenue Authority unless the law requires it.

## Good to know

**"I have to register a business first."**
No. You register yourself, using your own name and National Insurance number, not a business.

**"It's just another tax."**
It's protection that pays you back when you can't work. NIS does not share your details with the Barbados Revenue Authority unless the law requires it.

**"My income is too uneven to pay every month."**
You can pay weekly, monthly or in lump sums — pay more in good months and less in slower ones. What matters is that the full amount for the year is paid by **15 January of the following year**.

**"I missed years — it's too late for me."**
You may be able to pay for up to 3 missed years. NIS adds a 5% charge for each year a payment is late, so check with NIS which years you can pay for first.

**"My savings are my insurance."**
National Insurance works alongside your savings. It replaces some income when you can't work, so your savings last longer.
